📚 Books, Courses & YouTube Channels

The complete list of resources outside of GitHub repos. Curated and ranked by learning value at each phase.

📖 Books — The Essential Reading List

📘 Phase 1: Beginner Books

1. System Design Interview Vol 1 — Alex Xu ★★★★★

  • Why: The clearest beginner explanation of every major system design topic
  • Difficulty: Beginner → Intermediate
  • Length: ~300 pages
  • Use: Read cover-to-cover; do every problem on paper
  • Companion: ByteByteGo YouTube (same author)

2. Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces — Arpaci-Dusseau ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • Why: Best free OS book on the internet
  • URL: https://pages.cs.wisc.edu/~remzi/OSTEP/
  • Use: Read Chapters 4-7 (processes), 25-30 (concurrency)

3. Use The Index, Luke! — Markus Winand ★★★★ (FREE)

  • Why: Best practical SQL indexing guide
  • URL: https://use-the-index-luke.com/
  • Length: ~200 pages

4. The Art of Scalability — Abbott & Fisher

  • Why: Business + tech perspective on scale
  • Difficulty: Beginner-friendly
  • Note: A bit dated but the concepts (AKF cube etc.) are evergreen

📗 Phase 2: Intermediate Books

5. System Design Interview Vol 2 — Alex Xu ★★★★

  • Why: Vol 1 covered the basics; Vol 2 covers advanced problems (proximity service, hotel reservation, advertising platform)
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Use: Read after Vol 1; do every chapter

6. Designing Data-Intensive Applications — Martin Kleppmann ★★★★★ (THE essential book)

  • Why: Possibly the single most-recommended book for backend engineers
  • Difficulty: Intermediate → Advanced
  • Length: ~600 pages, dense
  • Use: Read slowly. Take notes. Re-read sections.
  • Schedule: 12 weeks (1 chapter/week)
  • Critical chapters: 3 (Storage), 5 (Replication), 6 (Partitioning), 7 (Transactions), 9 (Consistency & Consensus), 11 (Streams)

7. Building Microservices — Sam Newman (2nd ed., 2021) ★★★★

  • Why: The textbook on microservices done right
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Use: Read if your work involves microservices; skip otherwise

8. Release It! — Michael Nygard (2nd ed.) ★★★★★

  • Why: The bible of resilience patterns. Where circuit breakers, bulkheads, timeouts came into the mainstream.
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Length: ~350 pages
  • Highlights: Stability patterns, anti-patterns, capacity planning

📕 Phase 3: Advanced Books

9. Database Internals — Alex Petrov ★★★★★

  • Why: Deep dive into B-trees, LSM-trees, replication, distributed DBs
  • Difficulty: Advanced
  • Length: ~400 pages
  • Use: Read after DDIA's storage chapters

10. Designing Distributed Systems — Brendan Burns

  • Why: Patterns for cloud-native applications
  • Difficulty: Intermediate-Advanced
  • Note: By a Kubernetes co-creator; opinionated and useful

11. Distributed Systems (3rd or 4th ed.) — Tanenbaum & Van Steen ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • Why: The textbook used by university courses on distributed systems
  • URL: https://www.distributed-systems.net/index.php/books/ds3/ (free PDF)
  • Difficulty: Advanced
  • Length: ~700 pages
  • Use: Reference + targeted reading; not cover-to-cover

12. Site Reliability Engineering — Google ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • Why: How Google runs production at scale
  • URL: https://sre.google/sre-book/table-of-contents/
  • Use: Critical chapters: 1-7 (foundations), 17 (testing), 22 (cascading failures)

13. The SRE Workbook — Google ★★★★ (FREE)

  • URL: https://sre.google/workbook/table-of-contents/
  • Use: Practical SLO and incident management

📙 Phase 4: Expert Books

14. Software Engineering at Google — ed. Winters, Manshreck, Wright ★★★★ (FREE PDF)

  • Why: Engineering practices at planet scale
  • URL: https://abseil.io/resources/swe-book
  • Use: Code review, testing, dependency management at Google scale

15. Staff Engineer — Will Larson ★★★★

  • Why: What it means to be a Staff+ engineer
  • Use: Career navigation, technical leadership

16. The Manager's Path — Camille Fournier ★★★

  • Why: Companion to Staff Engineer for those who lead engineering teams

🎓 Online Courses

MIT 6.824 — Distributed Systems ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • By: Robert Morris, Frans Kaashoek
  • URL: https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/6.824/
  • Lectures: YouTube playlist
  • Labs: 5 progressive Go labs (MapReduce, Raft, KV Server, Sharded KV)
  • Difficulty: Advanced graduate course
  • Time commitment: ~150-200 hours
  • Notes: The single best resource for serious distributed systems learning

CMU 15-445 / 15-721 — Database Systems ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • By: Andy Pavlo
  • URL: https://15445.courses.cs.cmu.edu/
  • Lectures: YouTube
  • Labs: C++ implementation
  • Use: 15-445 is intro; 15-721 is advanced (HTAP, query optimization)

Martin Kleppmann's Distributed Systems Course (Cambridge) ★★★★★ (FREE)

  • URL: https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/teaching/2021/ConcDisSys/
  • Lectures: YouTube playlist
  • Companion: DDIA book by the same author

Grokking the System Design Interview (educative.io) — PAID

  • URL: https://www.educative.io/courses/grokking-the-system-design-interview
  • Cost: ~$59 (often discounted)
  • Use: Excellent interview-specific course; modern variant available too

Grokking the Advanced System Design Interview (educative.io) — PAID

  • URL: https://www.educative.io/courses/grokking-adv-system-design-intvw
  • Use: Companion to the above; covers more advanced problems

AlgoMaster.io — Free + Paid

  • By: Ashish Pratap Singh (author of awesome-system-design-resources)
  • URL: https://algomaster.io/
  • Use: Lots of free articles; paid course is well-regarded

ByteByteGo (paid newsletter / course)

  • By: Alex Xu (System Design Interview author)
  • URL: https://bytebytego.com/
  • Use: Most current; great visualizations

Coursera — Cloud Computing Specialization (UIUC)

  • URL: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/cloud-computing
  • Difficulty: Intermediate-Advanced
  • Use: Academic perspective on distributed systems

Educative — Distributed Systems for Practitioners

  • URL: https://www.educative.io/courses/distributed-systems-practitioners
  • Use: Practical, hands-on

🎯 Recommended Reading Order

If you have 3 months:

  1. System Design Interview Vol 1 — Alex Xu
  2. donnemartin/system-design-primer (Anki cards daily)
  3. Watch ByteByteGo top 30 videos
  4. Read 10 articles from binhnguyennus/awesome-scalability

If you have 6-12 months:

  1. System Design Interview Vol 1 + 2 — Alex Xu
  2. Designing Data-Intensive Applications — Kleppmann (the central work)
  3. Release It! — Nygard
  4. donnemartin/system-design-primer (Anki cards)
  5. karanpratapsingh/system-design (Chapters I-V)
  6. ByteByteGo + Hussein Nasser YouTube
  7. Build 2-3 projects

If you have 1-2 years (serious commitment):

  1. Everything above
  2. Database Internals — Petrov
  3. MIT 6.824 — implement Raft
  4. Martin Kleppmann's Cambridge course
  5. Read 30 papers from papers-we-love
  6. Read 100 engineering blog posts
  7. Contribute to an open-source distributed system
